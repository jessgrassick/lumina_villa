Local Guide

Things to do in Canggu

Canggu has changed fast. It is still Bali, but with better coffee. Here is what to know before you arrive.

Surfing

Canggu is Bali's surf capital, and it earns the title. Echo Beach handles experienced surfers: the reef break gets serious in dry season. Berawa is more forgiving and ideal for beginners, with consistent waves and plenty of instructors. Board rental from IDR 50,000 per day. Lessons start from IDR 350,000 and our concierge can arrange everything, including transport to the beach and equipment.

The surf culture here runs deep. At 6am, the water is full. By 9am, the warungs are packed with people drying off over Nasi Goreng and cold Bintangs.

Eating and Drinking

Canggu's food scene is legitimately excellent. At the bottom end, you will eat the best Nasi Campur of your life for under $2 at a roadside warung. At the top, you will find restaurants that hold their own against any major city. The brunch culture is huge: Crate Cafe for the atmosphere, Milk and Madu for the food, Hungry Bird for the best coffee. All within 10 minutes of the villa.

For sundowners, you have options. Old Man's at Batu Bolong is the Canggu institution: cold Bintang, live music, surf views. La Brisa is the spot for something slower. The Lawn does a proper sunset over the rice paddies. All are within easy scooter distance. The noise stays there; you come home to quiet.

Yoga

The yoga scene in Canggu is world-class and surprisingly affordable. The Practice Bali is the most well-known, with a stunning bamboo studio, multiple styles daily. Samadi Bali has a strong community and excellent teachers. Desa Seni doubles as a beautiful eco-resort with a full yoga programme. Drop-in classes run from IDR 150,000. Weekly unlimited passes are available at most studios.

If you would rather not leave the villa, our concierge arranges private morning sessions on the pool deck from IDR 225,000. The rice field backdrop helps.

Nightlife

Canggu does not pretend to be Seminyak, and that is the point. The nightlife here is lower key and better for it. Old Man's (Batu Bolong) is the anchor: cold beer, live music, open air. Deus Ex Machina does the cafe-meets-motorbike-shop-meets-music-venue thing better than anywhere else on the island. La Laguna is fairy lights and creative cocktails in an old fishing boat graveyard. Finns Beach Club if you want something bigger.

Everything is roughly 10 minutes from the villa by scooter. The neighbourhood itself is quiet. You hear the geckos, not the music.

Day Trips from Canggu

The villa is well positioned for exploring beyond Canggu. Tanah Lot is 20 minutes away: a sea temple on a coastal rock stack, best at sunset. Ubud is 60 minutes: rice terraces, the monkey forest, the art market, and some of Bali's best restaurants. Uluwatu is 90 minutes south: dramatic clifftop temple and the Kecak fire dance at sunset. Nusa Penida is a half-day boat trip: turquoise water, Kelingking cliff, manta rays at Manta Point. Mount Batur is a 2.5-hour drive for the sunrise trek: 4am start, back by noon.

Our concierge arranges private car and driver for any of these from IDR 600,000 per day, or full tours from IDR 900,000+ including guide and entrance fees.

The dry season from April to October is widely considered the best time to visit Canggu and Bali generally. Days are sunny, surf is consistent, and outdoor activities are easy to plan. July and August are peak season: lively, busy, and the most social time to be in Canggu.

That said, Bali is genuinely warm year-round, sitting between 27 and 30 degrees Celsius in most months. The wet season (November to March) brings afternoon showers, usually an hour or two of heavy rain followed by clear skies, but also fewer tourists, lower villa rates, and a more local feel. Many experienced Bali travellers actually prefer the wet season for this reason. The rice fields are also at their most vivid green. Lumina Villa's pool and outdoor spaces remain enjoyable throughout the year.
